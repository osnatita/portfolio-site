The context

A business that runs on documents it can't search.

An independent music publisher administers a large catalog of contracts, royalty statements, sync licenses and advances. The knowledge that runs the business lives entirely in those documents, spread across three systems built for storage rather than for answering questions.

85K+
Contract PDFs, invoices and royalty rows across Dropbox, FileMaker and Vistex
3
Systems of record with no way to cross-reference one against another
0
Single sources of truth. The full picture lived only in someone's head

Contracts sat in Dropbox (~5,000 PDFs), invoices and quotes in FileMaker (~20,000), and royalties in Vistex (~60,000 rows). Each system answered part of a question; none of them answered a whole one.

The problem

What was broken.

These were structural problems. They cost management hours, let renewals and revenue slip, and slowed any decision that touched more than one system.

1
No single source of truth. There was no centralized view of an artist's contracts, royalties and sync history, so even routine questions meant opening three systems and reconciling by hand.
2
Management was the bottleneck. The team constantly asked leadership for basic information that should have been self-service, turning a handful of people into a human search engine.
3
Renewals slipped through. Contract tracking was manual, so deals quietly approached expiry with no alert, and renewal opportunities were missed.
4
No room for a confident wrong answer. This is legal and financial data. On a split or a recoupment balance, a tool that answers without proof is a liability.
Discovery & research

Five teams, five versions of the same question.

I mapped the recurring questions each function asks the catalog, and what it costs them to answer today. The same need showed up everywhere: a fast, trustworthy answer that spans more than one system.

A&R

Prepping for an artist meeting means assembling contract terms, end dates, recent royalty performance and sync activity, pulled from three places by hand.

Business development

“Which contracts are coming up for renewal, and which are worth chasing?” There was no calendar, no pipeline, no alerts.

Sync licensing

Quoting a placement is guesswork without history: what did we charge for comparable genres, productions and territories?

Finance & royalties

Reconciling a payment means matching Vistex royalty data back to the contract terms and recoupment status that justify it.

Management

No live read on portfolio performance, team productivity, or where the risks sit: compliance, late payments, expiring deals.

The common thread

Across all five, the need was the same: a fast answer they could stand behind, pulled from more than one system at once.

The insight

The hard part was reasoning across systems, and proving it.

One test reframed the whole project. Take a simple question like “when does this contract expire?” Any document tool can answer that and cite the clause. The question that runs the business day to day is harder.

“How much do we owe this artist right now?” Answering that means cross-referencing the contract's recoupment terms against several quarters of Vistex royalty data and working out the unrecouped balance. No single system holds it. The answer has to be reasoned across all three, then proven.

The query that set the bar

So the design problem was making multi-system answers trustworthy: each figure traced to its source, scored for confidence, and visible only to the people allowed to see it. That set four principles I specced for the system's behavior.

01

Cite every figure

No answer appears without the exact contract clause, royalty period or ledger line it was drawn from. Grounding at the clause level, not the document summary, so every number is checkable.

02

Score the confidence

The system says how sure it is and what it couldn't reconcile, so a person knows when to double-check before acting. I set the confidence gate as a product decision.

03

Respect who can see what

Role-based permissions and an audit trail, because royalty and contract data isn't for everyone, and sensitive answers leave a record.

04

Answer in plain language

The team thinks in deals and payments, not queries, so the questions and answers stay conversational. A person confirms before anything is saved.

Product strategy · build vs buy

I evaluated eight tools against the real requirements.

The bar wasn't “can it chat with a PDF.” It had to reach all three systems, reason across them, cite at the level of a clause or a royalty period, score confidence, and enforce role-based access, all at the cost structure of a small team. That narrowed eight options to a tiered recommendation.

Microsoft 365 CopilotStrong once data is consolidated into the Microsoft graphGood, improvingBest balance: fits existing tooling, predictable per-seat costRecommended · enterprise
Custom Claude stackDeepest: purpose-built cross-referencing and recoupment logicCitations and confidence designed in from day oneHigher build, music-industry-specializedRecommended · premium
General chatbots / point toolsShallow; can't reach or reconcile the source systemsSummarize without provenanceCheap, but a liability on legal and financial dataRuled out

Also weighed: data consolidation and migration effort across 85K+ documents, API pricing volatility, and how each option handles permissions and audit on sensitive royalty data.

The product

One place to ask, three ways the answer shows up.

The product is a centralized, permissioned data layer with a plain-language interface on top. Beyond the direct question and answer, the same engine drives a live business dashboard and an always-on contract calendar.

The engine. Every answer is a retrieval (RAG) workflow reasoned across the three systems, scored, and either served with citations or flagged with exactly what it couldn't reconcile. A person confirms before anything is written back.

What it unlocks

What each team can do for itself.

The same cited, permissioned engine serves every function. Questions that used to route through management become self-serve.

A&R
Ready for any artist meeting

One profile: contract terms, end dates, recent royalties and sync activity. A portfolio view by contract status and upcoming renewals.

Business development
Catch every renewal in time

A contract calendar with automatic 30/60/90-day alerts, and a renewal pipeline with negotiation status and revenue forecasting.

Sync licensing
Quote from real history

Compare past quotes by genre, production, territory and budget, with pricing benchmarks and quote-to-placement conversion.

Finance & royalties
Reconcile quickly, with proof

Outstanding payments and recoupment status at a glance, and royalty reconciliation that ties Vistex data back to contract terms.

Management
A live view of the whole business

Portfolio performance, team productivity and revenue trends, plus a risk view: compliance, payment delays, renewal exposure.

Everyone
No more asking a colleague to look it up

Plain-language answers, cited and confidence-scored, with role-based access and an audit trail on sensitive data.
